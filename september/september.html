Studying in Asia has been a popular choice among students due to the close proximity to home while also obtaining world-class education. Yet with the increasingly competitive admissions officers, securing a spot in Asia’s top universities is getting harder.

Worry not! Our mentor, Sharon, who has known all the tricks of the trade, is sharing some steps to gain a seat in top Asian universities:

  • Step 1: Research and pay extra attention to admission requirements

    Universities in Asian countries usually have their own application platform and timeline (unlike US and UK with their Common App and UCAS, respectively), and therefore one university can really differ from another in terms of requirements.

    Schools can also have a different format for the same type of document (e.g. CV, Letter of Recommendation), so make sure you pay attention to these details (and don’t get them swapped between one and another!).

  • Step 2: Apply early rounds

    Universities usually have a larger quota for admission for students who apply early (deadline varies around November-December on the first semester of Grade 12). The chances of getting a scholarship is also higher, if not only in, the early application rounds.
