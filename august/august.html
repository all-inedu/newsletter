Ivy League universities and OxBridge have long been regarded as the best in the world of higher education. They're known as the places of academic excellence with historical significance. The thing is, how do you choose between them?

For starters, let’s prepare for both sets of institutions from Paul, an ALL-in mentor. Find out how to receive acceptance letters from Ivy League universities and OxBridge here:

  • Before you apply: Build a Personal Brand

    For Ivy League universities, you may try to attain juxtapositional depth / three-dimensionality to your personal brand. You can do this by holistically pursuing life inside and outside of school, including getting involved in extracurricular activities, participating as a volunteer, and landing internships.

    When it comes to Oxbridge, you can build your personal brand by excelling at school and conducting independent research. One simple way to get yourself informed about the latest news is by reading scholarly journals for breakfast.

  • When you apply: Write

    Preparing for your essay on Ivy League universities means that you can try to focus more on your identity, values, and idiosyncrasies. In this way, you'll come up with a well-rounded essay that reflects your personality and goals.

    On the other hand, Oxbridge universities tend to focus more on your academic and research track records.

Success Story

Currently, we are faced with two major problems: global warming and COVID-19 pandemic. Due to the on-going pandemic, people’s activities are restricted. When it comes to global warming, it’s something that people need to pay attention to. From these issues, Mellvin was inspired and started to make this bicycle project. This bicycle will not only help him stay fit during the pandemic, but the energy from pedaling the bike could also be converted to electrical energy. This project already solved three problems at once; keep people fit, produce clean energy, and power the gadgets in case a blackout occurs.

And now, Mellvin has created an electricity-generating bicycle using a 12V alternator, 500W pure sine wave inverter, and a 12V battery. He planned to redesign the bicycle and do further research on how to see how many calories are burnt on the monitor on the bicycle.
