As a high school student, chances are you’re faced with new challenges, more responsibility, and a rapidly changing environment. It could be the pressure of choosing the best-suit major in university. Or perhaps keeping up with your grades. Even with the most engaged parental support in place, things may unexpectedly fall through the cracks.

We know how overwhelming it can be as a high school student. Devi, an ALL-in mentor, is sharing her thoughts on mentors and their roles in helping high school students during their journey:

  • A confidence boost in moments of self-doubt

    When you're learning and trying new things, it can feel like going into the unknown. There might be periods of insecurity when you're feeling particularly unsure of yourself and your progress.

    Aside from answering the countless questions you might have in your mind, a mentor helps to put things into perspective. By sharing their experiences and reassuring you of your progress and growth, a mentor can help you stay out of your comfort zone.

  • Develop self-awareness and foster passions

    With only a few years left before navigating your own academic journey, you might feel left out if there is no definite path in mind.

    Here’s where a mentor comes into action: developing your self-awareness through guided reflection. From encouraging you to explore in and out of schools—through clubs, community programs and projects—or shortlisting areas to your interest, a mentor can help you understand yourself more, helping you to make a better-informed decision.

Success Story

Valencia joined ALL-in when she was already in Grade 12 and already had a lot of activities on her plate in the beginning, but not much related to her interest in biology and neuroscience. She followed through with suggestions of things she needed to do, including joining research competitions.

While her interests lay in STEM, she already has stellar writing skills that helped her a lot in her application. Valencia always responds fast and sends her revised essays quickly when it comes the time for the application process. Her personal branding as an aspiring neurologist who is keen to combat dyslexia through digital media and photography shines through her essays. Her hard work paid off when she landed into 13 top universities, including UPenn, UCLA, King’s College London, and University of British Columbia. She finally set out her decision to study at University of Pennsylvania as a Neuroscience student!
