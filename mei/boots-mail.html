If you're looking for a way to get involved in causes that you care about, you can start volunteering. However, it can feel like there's so much out there, and you don't know where to start. Don’t fret!

Sharon, our senior mentor here at ALL-in Eduspace, is sharing some tips revolving around volunteering. Find out how to get started with volunteering in the right way

  • Step 1: Start questioning your surroundings

    For you, life might have been mundane—whle actually there are a lot of unsolved issues around us. We might be able to do online schooling everyday without difficulties; how about those kids who can't afford gadgets and internet subscription? Start coming up with questions about your environment and you'll see some opportunities that may be relevant for you to volunteer for.

  • Step 2: Connect a cause to something you like

    Let's say you want to do something about climate change, but you are rather introverted. It might be too daunting for you to start volunteering by doing campaigns or starting debate clubs, and therefore you won't do it sustainably. Instead, choose an activity that you can enjoy—be it writing, drawing, teaching, and so on—and try to connect those activities with issues (or questions) you care for.

  • Step Step 3: Set up teams, break down goals

    Now that you know the activity and issue to address, you probably still think it's very complicated. You have one thing to build and one thing to break: your team and your goal, respectively.

His attitude of seizing every available opportunity shined during the university application period, where he aims for a scholarship in the US. With these scholarships —and a lot of hard work on his part—Darren is able to study at UC Berkeley with full scholarship!
