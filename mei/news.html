WHY VOLUNTEERING IS IMPORTANT?

So you're thinking about volunteering, but you're not sure if it's worth it. Or perhaps you wonder whether it is even the right thing for you. If you still have doubts, here are some things you might want to know about volunteering.

We all know, volunteering is a great way to give back and make a difference in the community. However, figuring out which volunteering opportunity suits you best can be tricky. You want to maximize the impact of your efforts—and that means finding the perfect fit.

Find more about university application and career exploration tips our blog!

If you're looking for a way to get involved in causes that you care about, you can start volunteering. However, it can feel like there's so much out there, and you don't know where to start. Don’t fret!

Sharon, our senior mentor here at ALL-in Eduspace, is sharing some tips revolving around volunteering. Find out how to get started with volunteering in the right way:

  • Step Step 1: Start questioning your surroundings

    For you, life might have been mundane—whle actually there are a lot of unsolved issues around us. We might be able to do online schooling everyday without difficulties; how about those kids who can't afford gadgets and internet subscription? Start coming up with questions about your environment and you'll see some opportunities that may be relevant for you to volunteer for.

  • Step Step 2: Connect a cause to something you like

    Let's say you want to do something about climate change, but you are rather introverted. It might be too daunting for you to start volunteering by doing campaigns or starting debate clubs, and therefore you won't do it sustainably. Instead, choose an activity that you can enjoy—be it writing, drawing, teaching, and so on—and try to connect those activities with issues (or questions) you care for.

  • Step Step 3: Set up teams, break down goals

    Now that you know the activity and issue to address, you probably still think it's very complicated. You have one thing to build and one thing to break: your team and your goal, respectively.

Success Story

Even when he was still in high school, Darren had set his eyes on political science and social issues. Aside from being actively involved in multiple extracurricular activities, including Model United Nations and the Student Council, he manages to fit in several work experiences.

While his impressive dedication to political science and social issues sounds serious, he’s a fun person who loves playing drums, swimming, and joking around with his peers.

His attitude of seizing every available opportunity shined during the university application period, where he aims for a scholarship in the US. All of his efforts paid off when he was awarded the Regents’ and Chancellor’s Scholarship and Koshland May Scholarship for UC Berkeley. With these scholarships—and a lot of hard work on his part—Darren is able to study at UC Berkeley with full scholarship!
