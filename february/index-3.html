Mentor tips
3 Career Exploration tips from
ALL-in Mentor

With countless occupations in the job market, finding a career that fulfills your interest and skills may sound overwhelming. We know that you may feel nervous about not turning your dream career into a reality, but don’t get all panicky!

Sharon, our senior mentor here at ALL-in Eduspace, is sharing her two cents on career exploration! If you’re still pondering over which career is the right fit for you, and not sure where to start your career exploration, check out these 3 tips from Sharon:

  • Interact with new people and be open-minded

    Meeting people with diverse backgrounds and careers is a great shortcut for your career exploration, since their experiences are real which helps you to do some fact and reality checks. Not to mention, two different persons having a similar occupation might have different career journeys, which will give you a wider perspective on the opportunities available in the workplace!

  • Get hands-on experience

    Trying to do activities or tasks that really correlates with the job not only helps you ensure whether you really can thrive in that particular area, but also helps you identify where your skill gaps are! If you are into investing, for example, try out some simulators and implement some techniques as you go.

  • Take some time to reflect

    Sometimes, being too busy with your activities leave you with no space to think whether you really want to pursue something in the long run. That’s why setting certain milestones and pair it with a reflection would help you stay on track. One simple thing you can do is sitting with someone who can mentor you and help you sort out your priorities.

Success Story

At the start of the pandemic, access to sanitary kits were limited due to the act of mask-hoarding by irresponsible actors, and those who lost their jobs couldn’t afford these supplies. Realizing this problem, Rayden began to develop Filtro with the goal of not only producing and selling custom masks, but also giving back to the community while tackling Indonesia's mask shortage problem.

70% of Filtro’s monthly profits goes to funding sanitary items, which consist of Filtro’s hand-painted masks, soap, and hand sanitizer. Throughout its existence, Filtro has partnered with non-profit organizations to raise funds as well as participated in several events, with their marketing reaching Singapore. Until today, Filtro is still selling their products and continues to donate sanitary kits to those in need.
