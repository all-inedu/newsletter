From building a meal generator using Javascript to testing the effect of ultraviolet radiation on fungal growth, the ideas of personal projects are limitless. We know you may feel worried about not turning your brilliant project ideas into a reality, but don’t sweat it!

Nic, an ALL-in mentor, is sharing the steps on how to start your own personal project. If you’re planning to make one but not sure how, have a look at 3 out of 5 steps from Nic:

  • Find the right supervisor

    A personal project, as the name suggests, is very much about you. Hence, make sure you're doing a project based on what you are interested in (or passionate in) rather than what everyone else is talking about.

  • Do a feasible study

    How do you want to feel once you've completed this project? What are the skills you'd like to build up? Starting a personal project means knowing what you want to achieve in the end. Therefore, it is important to establish your goal clearly before you start in order to keep yourself reminded of the small wins in your project.

  • Keep track of your secondary resources

    “Rome wasn’t built in a day.” SO DOES YOUR PROJECT!!

    Starting a robotic project does sound awesome—in theory. However, in reality, there’s a number of steps required to achieve your ultimate goals from your project, and they can be overwhelming sometimes. Instead of spending too much time thinking about every bit of your project in silence, break down the steps into smaller chunks and aim to complete these small chunks.

Success Story

As a high school student, Nathasya has her sights set in the field of biomedical engineering. Being a natural leader who wants to combine technology with healthcare, she has applied her stellar profile to the world’s top universities. With some acceptance letters already being mailed out, Nathasya has claimed hers from the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (with a scholarship), Imperial College, Hong Kong University, and getting shortlisted for an interview with Oxford University.

Even with her triumphs in university admissions, Nathasya has come a long way. From participating in STEM competitions, shedding light on underrepresented issues, and getting involved in her school's theater. Currently, she is focusing on her project called Life Online, a platform to educate students and the general public about life sciences.
