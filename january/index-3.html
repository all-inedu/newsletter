6 IB Extended Essay Tips from an IB
Examiner

The IB Extended Essay, or EE, is a mini-thesis you write under the supervision of an IB advisor that counts toward an IB Diploma. We know that you probably fear writing this, but worry not because the EE can be broken down into a manageable process with the right guidance.

This month’s tips came from Paul Edison, an IB examiner with 7 years of experience (and counting!), who is also our mentor here at ALL-in! For those of you who are currently in the middle of preparing your Extended Essay, check out 3 out of 6 tips from Paul:

  • Find the right supervisor

    A great supervisor can not only help with finding the right topic but also cheer you on when your experiment fails for the sixth time. They will be able to point you in the right direction when you're stuck, and they can help evaluate whether or not your argument is critical.

  • Do a feasible study

    Doing a thorough feasibility study before committing to your Research Question will save you so much time. For instance, before you even choose to analyze that exotic foreign film, make sure there are enough secondary sources out there that would help deepen your analysis. Spending an hour or two on feasibility studies might save you dozens of hours of research dead-ends and heartbreaks.

  • Keep track of your secondary resources

    Even as you read through journals and articles, it will be very tempting to just jot down quotes and leave the citation for a later date. Do NOT do this. Otherwise, you might find yourself having to find the source of a particularly useful quote in the pile of 40-50 different sources.

Success Story

Despite Indonesia’s abundance of sunlight, solar energy hasn’t fully shone in this developing nation. Brandon Darren, Dylan, and Rayandra saw this problem, and transformed it into a project of turning solar energy into a good help in our daily lives.

This led to the creation of PS2, with one promising solution in mind: a portable solar charger for smartphones and lamps. PS2 has conducted research on photovoltaic cells, potential model designs, and voltage regulation to generate the best overall cell brand. PS2’s optimism of making a change is unshakeable, given the fact that solar energy’s ultimate potential hasn’t gotten full attention here in Indonesia.
